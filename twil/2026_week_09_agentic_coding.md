[The agent-shaped world](../topics/AGI/2026-02-25_article_sunday-letters-from-sam_the-agent-shaped-world.md) landed as a clean framing: advantage comes from **agent-ready work** (interfaces, measurement, modularity), not AI hype.
So the question turned from “can it code?” to “can it stay coherent for hours?” → [Long horizon tasks with Codex (OpenAI Cookbook)](../topics/agentic_coding/2026-03-01_article_openai-cookbook_long-horizon-tasks-codex.md).

Once you zoom out to AGI timelines, you hit the uncomfortable policy trade: huge upside vs near-term tail risk. [Optimal Policies for AI? (Nick Bostrom)](../topics/AGI/2026-02-25_pdf_nick-bostrom_optimal-policies-for-ai.md) argues for **fast to capability**, then a **short, focused safety pause** (“swift to harbor, slow to berth”).
