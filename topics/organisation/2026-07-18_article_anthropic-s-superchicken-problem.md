# Anthropic's Superchicken Problem

*Cody Peterson — @SebaTheOracle*

## TL;DR

- Organizations often measure success one level too low: exceptional individuals or models do not guarantee a strong product or system.
- As frontier intelligence becomes less scarce, advantage shifts toward the whole customer experience—access, reliability, transparency, and trust.

## Highlights

- Anthropic does not need fewer brilliant people. It needs a broader definition of success, one that includes not only model intelligence and safeguard strength but also access, reliability, transparency, and trust.
- The real unit of performance was never the chicken. It was the coop.
- Culture is what a company’s systems repeatedly make easy—and what they repeatedly make difficult.

## My notes

The sentence about broadening success beyond intelligence and safeguards is the crux: product quality is a system property. A capable model still fails the user when access, routing, limits, communication, or reliability prevent the job from being finished.
