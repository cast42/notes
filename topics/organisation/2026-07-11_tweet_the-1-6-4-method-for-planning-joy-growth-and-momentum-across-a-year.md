# The 1-6-4 Method for planning joy, growth, and momentum across a year

*Ben Meer — @SystemSunday*

## TL;DR

- The 1-6-4 Method is a simple annual life-design framework for making sure the year contains both memorable experiences and steady personal growth.
- Its structure is one big year-defining challenge, six mini-adventures spread across the year, and four quarterly habits that compound over time.
- The method’s deeper point is that joy and growth often need to be scheduled deliberately, otherwise they get displaced by default digital routines and vague good intentions.

## Highlights

- "The 1-6-4 Method" organizes a year around 1 big event, 6 mini-adventures, and 4 quarterly habits.
- The big annual event is framed like a Misogi, a defining challenge that acts as the year’s anchor.
- The six mini-adventures are meant to create recurring anticipation and lived texture throughout the year.
- The four quarterly habits are the compounding layer, adding slow structural improvement rather than just memorable peaks.
- The key operational advice is to put all of it on the calendar so it becomes reality rather than aspiration.
