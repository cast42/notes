# America can dominate Shenzhen. A call for factory abundance. 

*Zane Hengsperger — @zanehengsperger*

## TL;DR

- The post argues that America can compete with Shenzhen not by nostalgia or a handful of flagship fabs, but by rebuilding a dense, abundant, software-native manufacturing base.
- Its playbook starts with culture, specifically restoring status to factory and trade work, then moves through factory density, energy strategy, domestic supply-chain completeness, and software/AI inside industry.
- The broader thesis is that reindustrialization is not just about policy or subsidies, but about combining cultural ambition, physical capacity, and technological leverage into a self-reinforcing industrial system.

## Highlights

- "America can dominate Shenzhen. A call for factory abundance."
- The post argues that Shenzhen’s edge is density, speed, and builder culture more than cheap labor.
- Its first and deepest bottleneck is cultural: America needs to make factory and trade work high-status again.
- The middle of the argument is about clustered factory abundance, energy abundance, and domestic supply-chain completeness.
- The final move is not software instead of factories, but software inside factories from day one.
