# We forgot how to build 

*Guido in 🇨🇳🇹🇼 — @Full_Metal_QR*

## TL;DR

- The post argues that visiting Chongqing makes China feel less like a copycat economy and more like a civilization that still knows how to build at scale, across infrastructure, energy, manufacturing, and increasingly AI.
- A central contrast is with Europe, which the author sees as drifting into overregulation, preservation, and stagnation while China compounds physical capacity and long-horizon planning.
- The AI angle is that if progress becomes more constrained by energy, chips, manufacturing, and execution than by pure software cleverness, China may be structurally stronger than many Western observers assume.

## Highlights

- "We just have to remember how to build."
- The piece frames Chongqing as a visceral reminder that China still builds upward, outward, and fast, while Europe has become more comfortable regulating than constructing.
- It links AI competitiveness to physical systems like power, semiconductors, rail, manufacturing depth, and national planning capacity.
- DeepSeek is used as a signal that China is no longer merely imitating on the AI layer.
- The broader thesis is not a precise forecast, but a warning that Western mental models of China are outdated.
