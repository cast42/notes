# Shift: Inside Nissan’s Historic Revival (Carlos Ghosn)

## TL;DR
Ghosn’s turnaround playbook at Nissan was radical simplicity: define a brutally clear, quantified, time-bound strategy; communicate it everywhere; and tie leadership credibility to transparent accountability. In an execution-fragmented organization, this created focus, speed, and urgency.

## Key takeaways
- **Make strategy brutally clear**: short, explicit, measurable commitments.
- **Quantify + time-box goals**: no vague transformation language.
- **Communicate repeatedly across all levels**: everyone should know goals, success criteria, and timelines.
- **Use accountability with transparency**: public commitments align behavior and raise execution discipline.
- **Break silo drag**: shared targets reduce slow consensus loops and cross-functional blockage.

## Nissan Revival Plan lens (as described)
- Return to profitability in 1 year.
- Reduce debt by half in 3 years.
- Hit explicit operating-margin targets.

## Why it worked (system perspective)
At Nissan, decision-making was consensus-heavy, siloed, and slow. The intervention reduced ambiguity and increased execution bandwidth by converting strategy into a small set of shared constraints and measurable outcomes.

## Nuance for modern orgs
This approach is strongest when the challenge is **operational execution** (profitability, debt, margin, cycle-time). It is less complete on its own for **exploratory problems** (innovation, platform bets, ambiguous AI strategy), where looser framing and optionality are often needed.
