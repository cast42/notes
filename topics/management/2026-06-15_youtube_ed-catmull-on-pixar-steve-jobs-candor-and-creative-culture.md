# Ed Catmull on Pixar, Steve Jobs, candor, and creative culture

*YouTube*

## TL;DR

- Ed Catmull argues that strong creative organizations are built by creating systems for candor, truth-telling, and collective problem-solving rather than relying on top-down brilliance.
- A major theme is how power distorts conversation, which is why Pixar carefully designed feedback settings like the Braintrust and kept overly dominant voices, including Steve Jobs, out of certain rooms.
- The talk also frames Pixar’s success as a cultural achievement: protecting difficult creative work, resisting ego and silos, and treating quality as the best long-term business strategy.

## Highlights

- "The discussion is always about the topic. It isn't about who's right."
- Catmull describes his leadership job as managing group dynamics so teams can actually hear hard truths and solve problems together.
- Steve Jobs was kept out of Braintrust meetings because his influence would distort the room too early.
- "Quality is the best business plan" is presented as an operating principle, not a slogan.
- Difficult problems are framed as valuable because they push teams toward originality rather than formula.
