# Copilot Cowork general availability and the agentic work layer in Microsoft 365

*Microsoft 365 Blog*

## TL;DR

- Microsoft is moving Copilot Cowork from preview to general availability and positioning it as an agentic work layer that can execute long-running, multi-tool business tasks end to end.
- The launch emphasizes enterprise grounding and controls, including Microsoft 365 security, compliance, audit, and admin-budget features, rather than just raw model capability.
- The broader product thesis is that Cowork sits above chat-style Copilot as the place where users define a job and the system runs it, with usage-based pricing and a future cost-optimized Cowork 1 model.

## Highlights

- "You define the work and Cowork runs it end-to-end and returns a completed result."
- The article frames Cowork as a full agentic work system, not just a chat assistant.
- Microsoft highlights three launch differentiators: enterprise trust boundaries, multi-model execution, and usage-based cost control.
- General availability adds admin controls, spending limits, plugin support, and a dedicated Cowork surface in the Copilot app.
- The strategic move is to make Cowork the agentic layer on top of Microsoft 365 Copilot.
