# Ideas over aesthetics in design reviews (Johnnie Manzari on Jony Ive)

## TL;DR
Strong design reviews are driven by **ideas and insights**, not visual polish alone. If the research uncovers a sharp point of view, even abstract presentations can be more productive than look-first demos.

## Key takeaways
- Aesthetics-first presentations can miss what matters.
- In this account, Jony Ive cared more about idea quality than surface styling.
- Research-backed, original insights can stand on their own, even before concrete form.
- Better reviews come from clear thinking, not just refined visuals.

## Source excerpt (captured via summarize)
Johnnie Manzari (@johnnie) on X, 2026-02-25:

> "Reading this reminded me that some designers would mistakenly assume that Jony cared primarily about aesthetics. They would lead a presentation with how things looked. Those presentations rarely went well in my opinion. He mostly cared about ideas. If you went in with research that lead to interesting insights--unique points of view that others had missed--you didn't even need to give those ideas concrete form. You could present them abstractly, and those meetings were far more interesting and productive."
