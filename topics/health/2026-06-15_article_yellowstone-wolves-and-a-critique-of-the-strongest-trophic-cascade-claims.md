# Yellowstone wolves and a critique of the strongest trophic-cascade claims

*ScienceDaily*

## TL;DR

- The article covers a reanalysis arguing that some of the strongest Yellowstone wolf trophic-cascade claims were overstated because of flawed statistical methods.
- Its core methodological criticism is circular reasoning: plant height was used both to calculate and to predict willow crown volume, which can manufacture an apparently strong result.
- The broader conclusion is not that wolves had no ecological effect, but that the effect is more modest, context-dependent, and spatially variable than the classic Yellowstone narrative suggests.

## Highlights

- "The relationship is circular."
- The reanalysis argues that the famous 1,500% willow crown-volume increase claim is not statistically credible as presented.
- Other issues cited include mismatched plots, unusual browsed willow forms, and underweighting hydrology and local site conditions.
- The authors argue for a more modest and spatially variable predator effect rather than a dramatic park-wide cascade.
- The bigger lesson is methodological: strong ecological claims require stronger statistical evidence.
