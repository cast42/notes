# Updated metabolic map and zoning definitions

*Iñigo San Millán — @doctorinigo*

## TL;DR

- Iñigo San Millán reframes training zones around metabolic stability rather than primarily substrate use or threshold labels.
- Zone 2 is defined as metabolic equilibrium near LT1/VT1, where lactate production and clearance stay tightly matched at the highest sustainable balance.
- Zones 3 to 4 are described as metabolic drift, and Zone 5+ as metabolic overload above the steady-state ceiling such as CP/MLSS/VT2/LT2/V4.

## Highlights

- "A lactate steady state is not the same as optimal metabolic equilibrium."
- Zone 1 = low metabolic stress, clearly below LT1/VT1.
- Zone 2 = metabolic equilibrium near LT1/VT1.
- Zones 3–4 = metabolic drift, moving away from optimal mitochondrial matching capacity even if lactate can still appear steady.
- Zone 5+ = metabolic overload above CP/MLSS/VT2/LT2/V4, where no true steady state is achievable.
