# Is Grep All You Need? post and paper

*elvis — @omarsar0*

## TL;DR

- A post by omarsar0 highlights a paper arguing that grep-style search, when wrapped in the right agent harness, can match or beat embedding-based retrieval on coding-agent tasks.
- The linked paper compares grep and vector retrieval across custom and provider CLI harnesses, and finds harness/tool-calling design matters a lot, with grep generally outperforming vector search in their setup.
- The practical takeaway is not that vectors are dead, but that primitive tools plus strong harness design may beat fancier retrieval stacks more often than expected.

## Highlights

- "It might be that what coding agents needed was not better embeddings. It was better harness design around primitive tools."
- Paper: Experiment 1 compares grep vs vector retrieval on 116 LongMemEval questions across Chronos, Claude Code, Codex, and Gemini CLI.
- Paper: Experiment 2 tests retrieval while adding unrelated conversation history to increase distraction and noise.
