# Apple in China: The Capture of the World's Greatest Company

## TL;DR

Patrick McGee traces how Apple moved manufacturing to China and helped suppliers develop advanced production skills. Apple's investment created an unmatched supply chain, but it also made the company dependent on China.

The book connects corporate decisions about cost and scale to industrial policy, technology transfer, and relations between China and the United States.

## Key takeaways

- Apple trained suppliers and workers to meet demanding production standards.
- The work helped China build skills and capacity across advanced electronics.
- Apple's supply chain became difficult to move because its strengths depend on dense local networks.
- The same system that powered Apple's growth created business and political risks.
