# Tim Cook personified big tech's maturity

*Andrew Sharp*

## TL;DR

- Andrew Sharp argues that Tim Cook’s Apple excelled at operational discipline, supply chain execution, services monetization, and shareholder returns, but represented a mature version of big tech rather than the category-creating magic associated with Steve Jobs.
- The piece frames Cook as an outstanding steward of scale and profit who consistently made rational decisions on China, services, regulation, and AI, even when those choices felt uninspiring or ethically compromised.
- Useful lens for leadership and innovation: great leadership at a mature company may look less like invention and more like optimization, risk control, and extracting advantage from an already-dominant position.

## Highlights

- Cook is portrayed less as a visionary product magician and more as a master allocator of advantage: operations, lobbying, supply chains, margins, and ecosystem leverage.
- The article contrasts Jobs-era Apple as a company selling possibility with Cook-era Apple as a company selling reliability inside an entrenched ecosystem.
- Sharp’s core bittersweet claim is that Cook did not just lead Apple well, he embodied what big tech became after the disruptive phase ended.
