Title: Writing is thinking

Main thesis:
The editorial argues that human-generated scientific writing is valuable not just for communicating results, but for thinking itself. In the age of large language models, scientists should be cautious about outsourcing writing, because doing so may weaken the reasoning, synthesis, and discovery that happen through the act of writing.

Key points:
- Writing is part of scientific thinking, not just reporting, and can help uncover new thoughts and ideas.
- The excerpt points to evidence that writing supports cognition, including learning and memory effects.
- LLMs can draft scientific text but are not true authors and raise questions about accountability and whether the writing reflects the researcher’s actual thinking.
- Model output can hallucinate or invent content, making verification costly.
- Limited assistance may be useful, but fully outsourcing writing risks losing reflection, refinement, and narrative shaping.
