Article: Microsoft says it has over 20M paid Copilot users, and they really are using it
Source: TechCrunch
URL: https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/29/microsoft-says-it-has-over-20m-paid-copilot-users-and-they-really-are-using-it/

Key extracted points:
- Microsoft says Microsoft 365 Copilot now has 20 million paid enterprise Copilot seats.
- Satya Nadella said Copilot queries per user were up nearly 20% quarter over quarter.
- Nadella claimed weekly engagement is now at the same level as Outlook, framing Copilot as a daily habit with intense usage.
- Microsoft says the number of companies paying for over 50,000 seats has quadrupled.
- Named large-seat customers include Bayer, Johnson & Johnson, Mercedes, and Roche, each with more than 90,000 seats.
- Accenture was highlighted as the largest Copilot win to date with more than 740,000 seats.
- Microsoft emphasized that Copilot is not tied to a single model and can route across multiple models, including support for Anthropic Claude in Microsoft 365.
- Agent mode was described as a key usage driver and had reportedly become the default experience across Copilot and Word, Excel, and PowerPoint.
- Microsoft recently made Copilot’s agentic capabilities generally available, letting Copilot take multi-step actions directly in documents.

Interpretation notes:
- The important claim is not just paid seats but claimed engagement parity with Outlook, which suggests Microsoft wants investors and buyers to see Copilot as habit-forming infrastructure, not experimental AI shelfware.
- The article leans on Microsoft’s own earnings-call framing, so the numbers are company claims rather than independent verification.
- The most strategically interesting part is the combo of distribution through M365, very large enterprise deals, and deeper agentic workflows inside existing productivity surfaces.
