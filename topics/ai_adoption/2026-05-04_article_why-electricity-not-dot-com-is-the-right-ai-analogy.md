# Why electricity, not dot-com, is the right AI analogy

*Joe Reis*

## TL;DR

- Joe Reis argues AI adoption looks more like electrification than the dot-com era: the core technology works, but most organizations have not redesigned their workflows, architecture, or org structure to capture its full value.
- The key mistake is bolting LLMs and agents onto legacy systems and processes, which creates visible activity without genuine transformation.
- The real gains will come from AI-native organizational redesign, where decision-making moves closer to operational contexts and the old centralized bottlenecks disappear or weaken.

## Highlights

- ‘We’re in 1905’ is the core metaphor: everyone has bought the motor, but almost nobody has rebuilt the factory.
- AI-assisted migration can be deceptive progress if it only reproduces old architecture faster and more cheaply.
- This is one of the clearer frames for why enterprise AI adoption can be simultaneously broad, expensive, and underwhelming in measured business impact.
