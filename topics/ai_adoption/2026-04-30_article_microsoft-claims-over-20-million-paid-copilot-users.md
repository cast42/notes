# Microsoft claims over 20 million paid Copilot users

*TechCrunch*

## TL;DR

- Microsoft says Microsoft 365 Copilot now has more than 20 million paid enterprise seats, a much bigger number than many skeptics expected.
- The more interesting claim is usage, not just seats: Satya Nadella said Copilot queries per user rose nearly 20% quarter over quarter and weekly engagement is now at Outlook levels.
- If true, this suggests AI adoption in the enterprise is being driven less by standalone chatbots and more by distribution inside existing productivity workflows plus increasingly agentic document actions.

## Highlights

- The article is mostly Microsoft’s own earnings-call framing, so the scale and engagement claims should be treated as company-reported rather than independently verified.
- Large enterprise rollouts, including Accenture at 740,000 seats, matter because they show Copilot is being sold as platform infrastructure, not just optional experimentation.
- Agent mode becoming default across Copilot and Office apps hints at the next phase of adoption: AI moving from answering questions to doing multi-step work inside documents.
