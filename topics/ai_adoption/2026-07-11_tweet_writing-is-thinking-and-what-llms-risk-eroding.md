# Writing is thinking, and what LLMs risk eroding

*Dr. Dominic Ng — @DrDominicNg*

## TL;DR

- The post argues that writing is not just a way to communicate thought but one of the main ways people develop it, so outsourcing writing to LLMs may train the mind toward passivity rather than clarity.
- The attached editorial extends that point into science, arguing that human-generated writing supports reasoning, synthesis, memory, and discovery in ways that are lost when text generation is fully delegated.
- The practical takeaway is not that AI should never assist writing, but that using it for total substitution can erode the very cognitive work that makes writing valuable.

## Highlights

- "Writing forces your brain to coordinate memory, reasoning, and meaning-making simultaneously."
- The post’s core contrast is that writing rewires the mind toward clearer thinking, while passive LLM delegation rewires it toward consumption.
- The attached editorial argues that writing is part of scientific thinking itself, not just a reporting layer.
- It warns that LLM-generated text may save time superficially while weakening reflection and increasing verification burden.
- The durable lesson is to use AI as assistance around writing, not as a wholesale replacement for the thinking-writing loop.
