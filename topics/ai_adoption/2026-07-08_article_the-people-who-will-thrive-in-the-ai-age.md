# The people who will thrive in the AI age

*The Atlantic*

## TL;DR

- The article argues that in an AI-saturated world, the key differentiator will not be raw intelligence but whether people use AI to avoid thinking or to stretch their own capacities.
- It warns that AI can intensify work and erode attention, memory, motivation, and critical judgment when people drift into convenience and over-deference.
- Its hopeful model is the person who treats AI as a tool for agency and learning, using it to support hard thinking rather than replace it.

## Highlights

- "When intelligence is plentiful, volition is valuable."
- The article’s main dividing line is relationship to mental effort, not IQ.
- It warns of "AI brain fry," cognitive surrender, and a broader risk of cognitive polarization.
- The strongest users are framed as people who use AI for hints, scaffolding, and extension rather than passive answer consumption.
- The broader cultural recommendation is to cultivate autonomy, competence, and willingness to do difficult mental work.
