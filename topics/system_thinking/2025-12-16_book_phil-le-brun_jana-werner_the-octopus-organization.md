# The Octopus Organization (Phil Le-Brun, Jana Werner)

## TL;DR
A systems-oriented leadership book arguing that organizations should evolve from rigid, top-down transformation programs toward continuous, adaptive change. The “octopus” metaphor emphasizes distributed intelligence, flexibility, and resilience.

## Key ideas
- Move from episodic “big-bang” transformation to continuous adaptation.
- Replace bureaucracy and control-heavy structures with trust, learning, and faster feedback.
- Favor many small, compounding changes over centralized one-off programs.
- Make organizational design support execution, not just governance.
