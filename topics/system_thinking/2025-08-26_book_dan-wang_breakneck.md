# Breakneck: China's Quest to Engineer the Future

## TL;DR

Dan Wang describes China as an engineering state that builds at great speed and scale. He contrasts it with the United States, where legal rules and process often slow construction and reform.

The book also covers the human cost of China's approach. Wang argues that each country needs some of the other's strengths.

## Key takeaways

- China treats engineering and construction as core tools of government.
- The United States is strong at protecting rights but often struggles to build.
- Fast state action can deliver infrastructure while also causing severe harm.
- A better system would combine the ability to build with strong individual rights.
