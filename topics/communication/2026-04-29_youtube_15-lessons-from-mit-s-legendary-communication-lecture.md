# 15 lessons from MIT's legendary communication lecture

*Gdv2025*

## TL;DR

- This video distills Patrick Winston’s classic MIT lecture on speaking: presentation skill is a core multiplier for ideas, and effective talks are built from learnable structure and technique rather than charisma.
- Key lessons include opening with an empowerment promise, helping audiences repeatedly re-enter the talk, reducing slide clutter, pacing with boards or props when possible, and ending on concrete contributions instead of filler.
- The deeper point is that communication is not packaging added after the fact, it is part of how ideas survive, get recognized, and become influential.

## Highlights

- Winston’s priority order is memorable: ability to speak, ability to write, and quality of ideas, in that order for professional impact.
- His slide advice is mostly subtraction, because dense slides compete with the speaker for the audience’s limited language processing attention.
- For job talks and persuasive communication, memorable work needs packaging: a symbol, slogan, surprise, salient idea, and story.
