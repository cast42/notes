# Codex subscription and usage growth versus Claude Code

*Latent Space / AINews*

## TL;DR

- The article’s main value is the growth graph: it stitches together disclosed Codex user milestones to show a steep 2026 adoption curve, from roughly 550k to 700k users at the start of the year to 2M in March, 6M on July 12, and 7M by July 13.
- That implies roughly 10x user growth in about six months, making Codex one of the clearest recent examples of explosive subscription and usage acceleration in AI coding tools.
- For comparison, the piece pairs this with Anthropic’s earlier Claude Code disclosure of roughly 2M users and $2.5B ARR in February, while warning that the products are not perfectly comparable.

## Highlights

- The note is mainly about subscription and usage data, not product features.
- Codex datapoints cited: ~550k to 700k users on Jan 1, 2M in March, 6M by July 12, and 7M by July 13.
- The article frames this as >10x growth in six months.
- Claude Code comparison point: roughly 2M users and $2.5B ARR in February.
- The growth chart is useful because public subscription and active-user disclosures for AI coding services are still rare.
